Jon,

In the course of other discussions, I discovered that there are "guest" accounts on soda for people who are not Berkeley students. We seem not to have a specific policy on this, so I have enunciated one, and this is to inform you (and hence XCF) of it:

I have no objections to limited guest accounts on [studentadministered machines in EECS's jurisdiction], as long as the appropriate faculty sponsor ... is informed of (and consents to) each such guest, the guest roster is periodically reviewed, the accounts cause negligible impact on our facilities, and are not put to commercial, illegal, or scandalous use. This notification can be informal (e.g., a periodic e-mail list of such accounts), and "consent" can be informal (e.g., ``assume I consent unless you hear otherwise.'')

It is necessary (I believe) that we have an official policy of monitoring any such account, or I can forsee oodles of trouble if it turned out that we allowed anyone off the street to get an account without supervision from the responsible authorities of any kind. In any case, I certainly expect that there will be very few such accounts.

P. Hilfinger
